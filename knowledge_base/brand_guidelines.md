# MarginIQ — Brand & Discount Depth Guidelines (Synthetic)

## Purpose

This document governs how deep and how often premium and luxury products may be
discounted, to protect price image and long-run margin. The critic applies these
guidelines when brand signals are present; the luxury strategist cites them when
arguing against deep cuts.

## Tiering

| Tier | Examples | Max routine discount | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Luxury | premium gelato, artisan / imported lines | 15% | Deeper cuts require clearance justification |
| Premium | brand-name frozen desserts, specialty | 20% | Avoid back-to-back deep promos |
| Mainstream | grocery staples, commodities | 35% | Price-led category, brand risk low |

## Reference-price protection

A discount that pushes the shelf price more than **20% below the 90-day reference
price** on a premium or luxury item is flagged as brand-dilutive. Repeated dilutive
promotions train customers to wait for deals and erode willingness to pay at full
price.

## Promotion frequency

- Premium and luxury items: no more than **one deep promotion (>15%) per 8 weeks**.
- Frequent shallow promotions are preferred over occasional deep ones for premium
  brands, because they protect the reference price.

## Dilution risk threshold

When the brand model reports a dilution risk above **0.55**, the critic should
favor the shallowest discount that still meets the demand and inventory objectives,
and the luxury strategist should argue explicitly for price-image protection.

## Override

These guidelines are overridden only when the clearance policy applies — that is,
when perishable stock is at genuine risk of spoiling. In that case avoiding waste
takes priority over reference-price protection. See clearance_policy.
